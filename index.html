Schedule

  • May 31, 2014
    Los Angeles City Hall
    Innovation Conference Track

    Civic innovation and engagement, the Los Angeles tech economy, the digital divides, urban narratives, and alternative reality games.

     

    10:20am – 11:20am

    Panel 1: Digital Divides

    This panel will discuss the “digital divides” and how we will address them. The digital world is more important today than ever before. Having access to the Internet, participating in the tech revolution, and being able to use online and mobile services are essential for modern life. Despite this, Los Angeles ranks 2nd from the bottom in California for Internet access and use with between 14 and 30% of residents not being on the Internet (source: CETF). Women’s participation in the tech industry remains low and the US overall is said by some to be falling behind. Helping everyone cross the various digital divides is crucial to the success of our city and its residents.

     

    11:20am-11:40am

    Keynote: The Tech Economy
    Amy Amsterdam, Director of Innovation at LAEDC
    Michelle Garakian, Mayor’s Office of Economic Development, City of Los Angeles

     

    11:40am-12:40pm

    Panel 2: Alternative Realities, Narratives, and Games in the City

    This panel will explore how digital games are evolving and increasingly intersecting with the city around us. They’re even changing how we experience the city. Developers of all sizes are making games for smartphones and tablets that push new boundaries: alternative reality games (ARGs), augmented reality games, treasure quests, and mobile social local games. There are even games which trigger the environment around you to change. We will explore whether we can use games to change how people move through the city? Can games open our eyes to seeing new things? How can the city use games to address our challenges of traffic, sustainability, and development? What can the City of Los Angeles do to help game developers create new experiences?

     

    12:40pm-1:30pm

    Lunch
    Tech Fair
    Jobs Fair

     

    1:30pm-1:50pm

    Keynote: Los Angeles in 2050
    Tara Roth, President, Goldhirsh Foundation

     

    1:50pm-2:50pm

    Panel 3: Los Angeles Tech Economy

    The Los Angeles technology sector has been exploding over the last couple of years. What’s causing this growth? What unique challenges do we face in Los Angeles? How will we continue to build LA as the fastest growing sector for tech success? Come join a candid conversation from the various stakeholders: entrepreneurs, investors, technologists, and advisors on what unique strengths, weaknesses and challenges the LA ecosystem presents.

     

    2:50pm-3:50pm

    Panel 4: Civic Innovation and Engagement

    In a city of four million, we’ve come a long way from town hall democracy. Over the decades, we’ve built a huge organization to provide city services—and citizens feel increasingly disconnected and dissatisfied with an impersonal and complicated government. How can we re-imagine a partnership with citizens and public servants to seize the opportunities of the 21st Century? What needs to change in local government? What timeless principles need to be re-embraced? A diverse panel of civic and municipal innovators will tackle the potential for a more nimble, responsive and collaborative approach to making Los Angeles safe, prosperous, livable and sustainable in the competitive global economy. Are we ready to embrace community policing, social media, empowering the workforce and streamlining bureaucracy? What stands in the way of making Los Angeles the most well-run big city in America? What will it take to overcome the challenges—and what promising efforts are showing the way?

  • May 31, 2014
    Los Angeles City Hall
    Technology Conference Track

    Technology shifts are happening everywhere in our city. This track will discuss big data, connected cars, green vehicles, autonomous automobiles, and, of course, the connected city.

     

    10:20am-11:20am

    Panel 1: Mobility and Future of Transportation in Los Angeles

    This interdisciplinary panel will catalyze an inspiring conversation about the future of mobility in Los Angeles. Experts in the areas of transportation design, technology, engineering, urban planning, energy, and community engagement will discuss how Los Angeles can best solve the major transportation challenges facing our city including congestion, public safety, and air pollution. Participants will reveal powerful roles for technology and innovation to shape the future of mobility in a connected and sustainable city.

     

    11:20am-11:40am

    Keynote by Motorola Solutions

     

    11:40am-12:40pm

    Panel 2: Big Data, Open Data, and Visualization

    Big data is changing how we live in the city. The LAPD has used big data, for example, to help drive crime rates in Los Angeles to levels not seen since the early 1950s. Google Maps and Waze help commuters navigate traffic daily using crowd-sourced and city-provided big data. Neither of these would be possible without seamless ubiquitous access to data unavailable in years past. The City of Los Angeles is now publishing more than 100 datasets on everything from budget numbers to traffic statistics, from the location of parking meters to the location of crime reports. How will big data, the visualization of big data, and sensors change our lives over the coming years? How do we manage privacy? What innovative uses can we imagine where our apps and services are able to access data that have previously been hard or impossible to obtain? What data from the City of LA do we need to find and publish next?

     

    12:40pm-1:30pm

    Lunch
    Tech Fair
    Jobs Fair

     

    1:30pm-1:50pm

    Keynote by Microsoft Corporation

     

    1:50pm-2:50pm

    Panel 3: The Los Angeles Autonomous Vehicle Future

    The race is on to introduce self-driving cars to LA. Will you own an autonomous car in the future that does the driving for you? Or will you sell your car and catch rides everywhere from a future Uber service offering a vast “robo-taxi” service across LA? Either way, there are efficiencies to be had with this future, and will enable LA to become an even better place to live. Leading technologists, designers, and an architect will discuss where this technology is today, when we can expect to see it LA, and share a few scenarios of how this future will rollout. In addition, the autonomous vehicle future may develop in “special mobility zones” that limit larger automobiles. Both neighborhoods and downtown centers have begun discussing these future opportunities. The panel will discuss this opportunity to “right-size” autonomous vehicle systems as well. There are many forms this future of mobility might take, but all of them are amazing!

     

    2:50pm-3:50pm

    Panel 4: The Connected City

    A city is a physical, cultural, communal, social, mobile, and digital experience. Los Angeles is a city its residents and visitors connect with through culture, by walking, by traveling on vehicles and bikes, with media, and over the Internet. In short, LA is wonderful, complex, and interconnected. This panel will explore how technology is changing how we relate to our city and its many aspects. How is architecture accommodating personal mobility with smartphones? What happens when the buildings, people, vehicles, and things are connected wirelessly in the “Internet of Things?” How will urban planning incorporate online communities? How can the city use technology to bring better livability, sustainability, and resiliency? What will a “city” be in the future where every person is connected the cloud, connected to each other over social networks, and connected electronically to the physical city itself?
